My name is David Sanchez Cano. I was born in Puebla, Mexico and moved to the United States when I was three. I was raised in Los Angeles ever since. My parents taught me the importance of hard work at a young age. This work ethic has helped me at young age. I was constantly praised for being a good student by my elementary, middle, and high school teachers.

After graduating high school with a 4.04 I had to choose between UC Santa Barbara and UC Riverside. I decided to go to UC Riverside because that means I will still be able to visit my family. Choosing a major was also difficult. I knew that English was not my best field. In that case, I decided to steer away from majors that would require a lot of reading/writing. My favortite subject was math. However, I did not have a good experience with science classes so I wasn't sure if a STEM major was for me.

I decided to pick the major of Business Economics. I have learned a lot about economics in my first year at UC Riverside. I had really good professors for Macroeconomics and Microeconomics. A career that caught my attention was the work that was done in the Federal Reserve System and the United States Department of Treasury. Being an economic advisor to help a small town, city, state, or the entite contry was awe inspiring.
